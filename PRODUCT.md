# Product Context

## Register

brand

## Product

MNIPH is Pin-Hao's personal writing space and digital garden. Its central metaphor is a Pensieve: a place to deposit thoughts, return to them later, and notice how technical work, learning, relationships, interests, and self-understanding change over time.

This is not primarily a portfolio, résumé, lead-generation page, or personal-brand landing page. Professional work belongs here, but it should coexist naturally with unfinished questions, music, tennis, travel, career exploration, and ordinary life.

## Purpose

- Leave a durable trace of what Pin-Hao is thinking, learning, making, questioning, and living through.
- Make publishing unfinished thoughts feel legitimate and low-pressure.
- Help Pin-Hao's future self notice recurring interests, changed opinions, answered questions, and work that actually happened.
- Let another reader enter through any topic and gradually discover connections between technical and personal writing.

## Audience

### Primary

Pin-Hao's future self: someone returning months or years later to understand what mattered, what changed, and which questions kept returning.

### Secondary

- People with similar uncertainty about technical work, career, action, learning, and life direction.
- Friends, researchers, engineers, collaborators, and strangers who want to understand Pin-Hao as a whole person rather than a single professional role.
- Readers who may arrive through cryptography and stay for personal writing, or arrive through life writing and discover the technical work.

## Desired Relationship

Do not persuade visitors to choose, hire, or contact Pin-Hao. Invite them to understand what he is noticing. The preferred journey is:

1. Arrive anywhere.
2. Begin reading without orientation friction.
3. Follow a related topic, question, or memory.
4. Form a fuller impression over time.
5. Return later without being pushed through a conversion funnel.

## Voice

- **Thoughtful:** precise enough to be useful without pretending every thought is resolved.
- **Candid:** personal, specific, occasionally self-deprecating, and never corporate.
- **Open-ended:** comfortable with uncertainty, revision, contradiction, and unfinished work.
- **Playful:** small jokes and personal references are welcome when they feel natural.

Write in Pin-Hao's natural mix of Traditional Chinese and English. Do not normalize the voice into formal marketing copy or generic technical blogging.

## Content Model

The Pensieve holds four kinds of entries:

- **Note:** a short observation, reaction, experiment, or unfinished thought.
- **Essay:** a longer piece with more structure, without requiring a permanent conclusion.
- **Question:** something worth returning to repeatedly.
- **Memory:** a life record involving people, music, travel, sports, or a specific moment.

Entries may carry an updated date when they are meaningfully revised or revisited. Show that concrete history rather than asking readers to interpret an abstract status system.

## Design Intent

The site should feel quiet, readable, personal, and easy to wander through. It should allow technical and personal material to sit beside each other without forcing them into one professional identity. Content should feel accumulated rather than merchandised.

## References

- BTS *PROOF*: archival structure, black-and-white restraint, catalog-like identity, and the feeling of accumulated history.
- Jungkook's former Instagram naming: a personal, playful reference behind the MNIPH identity.
- Maggie Appleton's digital garden: visible learning, linked ideas, and permission for notes to evolve.
- A well-used research notebook: dates, annotations, traces of revision, and ideas at different levels of maturity.

References guide tone and structure. Do not copy official artwork, logos, album graphics, or a recognizable branded composition.

## Anti-References

- Startup and SaaS landing pages.
- Large marketing heroes and slogan-first layouts.
- Résumé-first portfolios and prominent contact calls to action.
- Generic personal-brand copy, achievement theater, and conversion language.
- Purple gradients, glassmorphism, cyberpunk hacker imagery, nested cards, and icon tiles above every heading.
- Excessive polish that makes unfinished thoughts feel unwelcome.
- Taxonomies that force every interest to justify one professional identity.

## Success Criteria

- A visitor can start reading within a few seconds.
- Pensieve entries, not navigation or self-promotion, hold the visual attention.
- Notes and unfinished questions look intentional rather than incomplete.
- Technical and personal writing feel like parts of the same person.
- The interface encourages gentle discovery without demanding a next action.
- Pin-Hao feels comfortable publishing before a thought is fully resolved.
